Top Eco-Driving Tips

Simple changes to your driving habits can lead to significant environmental and financial benefits

Smooth Acceleration & Braking

Gentle acceleration and anticipating stops can reduce fuel consumption by up to 20%. Practice gradual acceleration and coast to decelerate when safe.

Learn more →

Maintain Optimal Speed

Most vehicles achieve optimal fuel efficiency between 45-65 mph. Using cruise control on highways helps maintain a steady, efficient speed.

Learn more →

Reduce Idle Time

Turn off your engine when stopped for more than 30 seconds. Modern vehicles use less fuel restarting than idling for extended periods.
